About the Exam

Class A/B License Requirements

Virginia requires two licenses to become a contractor: Business / Law and Technical. This Class A/B license covers the business and law portion and is required by the designated employee or member of responsible management.

You must complete 8-hour Pre-License requirement for all new Virginia Contractors. The same person who takes the Pre-License course must be the same person who takes the Class A/B exams.

Class A

Required for all projects in exceeding $120,000 and yearly revenue over $750,000. The Company must have a $45k minimum net worth

Class B

Required for all projects exceeding $10,000 and not more than $120,000 per project, and not to exceed $750,000 in yearly revenue. The company must have a minimum net worth of $15,000

Exam and Application: Exams are administered by PSI and can be scheduled online at www.psionoline.comor by calling PSI EXAMINATION SERVICES (800) 733-9267, or in alternative, call Casaflex and we will make the appointment for you.

Class-A

Exam consists of three parts: Advanced, Virginia and General.

Class-B

Exam consists of two parts: Virginia and General portions.

REMEMBER: Once you passed the tests for Class A or B and submit you application for licensure and when approved by the DPOR you may take the SPECIALTY test to obtain your license.

BOOKS

The NASCLA Code Virginia 11th Edition and the Virginia Construction

To pass the Class A or Class B contractor exam in Virginia, the only books you need are the NASCLA Contractor’s Guide to Business, Law and Project Management, Virginia 11th Edition, and the Virginia Construction Code.

Once you pass the exam, you can apply for your license and receive approval to take the technical (specialty) exam. Your contractor license will be issued after you successfully pass the specialty exam.
